Cuba

Pretty, Nice, "Podrido"

Cuba is a Caribbean island nation under communist rule. It has sugar-white beaches and is dotted with tobacco fields, which play a part in the production of the country's legendary cigars. The capital, Havana, is lined with pastel houses, 1950s-era cars and Spanish-colonial architecture in the 16th-century core, Old Havana. Salsa music plays in the dance clubs and cabaret shows are performed at the famed Tropicana.

Useful tips:

  • IF YOU ARE AMERICAN BRING CASH! American credit/debit cards do NOT work here at all. The Canadians and the rest of the world are in luck.
  • Don't be scared of trying food from hole in the wall spots! Most tourists try all the nice restuarants (which are still relatively dirt cheap by most standards) many ordinary Cuban citizens eat from local spots.
  • Do not hesitate to ask for help. Unlike most of the world Cubans are extremely friendly and willing to go out of their way to help.
  • If you need the internet then stock up on internet cards! The internet is state run and can only be accessed via internet cards that can be purchased from ETECSA shops.
  • Taxis can be quite expensive so try out to the busses("guaguas") or the communal taxies (taxis comunales)
