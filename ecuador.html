Ecuador

Tropical paradise with a twist!

Ecuador is a country straddling the equator on South America's west coast. Its diverse landscape encompasses Amazon jungle, Andean highlands and the wildlife-rich Galapagos Islands. In the Andean foothills at an elevation of 2,850m, Quito, the capital, is known for its largely intact Spanish colonial center, with decorated 16th- and 17th-century palaces and religious sites, like the ornate Compania de Jesus Church.

Useful tips:

  • Bring cash! Credit and debit cards are widely accepted throughout the country but beware of international fees. Plenty of fun stuff such as street foods and souvenirs are usually cash only so carrying some with you helps.
  • Don't be scared of trying hole in the wall food spots! Food in Ecuador can be extremely cheap and tasty but you have to look outside tourists areas and into the local zones.
  • Renting a car might be helpful. There are plenty of busses that travel long distances but if you want to be truly free to do and go where you want then a car is best for you.
  • Try speaking Spanish! Ecuadorian spanish is known to be as some of the easiest to learn given how it sounds and its fluidity. (It usually scores you points with the locals!) Be aware that the accent on the coast is differnt than the coast more inland.
  • ECUADOR USES THE AMERICAN DOLLAR! If you are American like me you have it easy when it comes to money since they use the exact same currency as we do.
